Shao-Yuan Lo   羅紹元

I am a 2nd year PhD student in Electrical and Computer Engineering at Johns Hopkins University, advised by Prof. Vishal M. Patel. I spent a wonderful time as an Applied Scientist Intern at Amazon Lab126 , working with Wei Wang and Jim Thomas. I received my M.S. (Electronics Engineering) and B.S. (EECS Honors Program) degrees from National Chiao Tung University, and worked with Prof. Hsueh-Ming Hang.

My research area lies in computer vision and machine learning. Currently, my research is focusing on adversarial machine learning. I am exploring novel adversarially robust methods for computer vision problems, such as image classification, video recognition and novelty detection. I also work on domain adaption methods for monocular depth estimation during my internship at Amazon Lab126. During my master's education, I was working on efficient semantic segmentation algorithms for self-driving applications.
